QUESTION FRAMEWORK

The intake profile is generated through a structured questionnaire, composed of 9 fixed questions. Each question is designed to extract behavioral tendencies, not personal preferences, in order to build a reliable and function-oriented eating pattern.

The questions are grouped into three functional categories:

LIFESTYLE PREFERENCES ›
MEAL STRUCTURE PREFERENCES ›
FLAVOR PREFERENCES ›

Lifestyle Preferences

×
Q1

HOW WOULD YOU DESCRIBE YOUR DAILY FOOD CHOICES?

Assesses overall decision-making tendencies in food selection, from stability to spontaneity.

Q2

WHAT'S YOUR TYPICAL BREAKFAST LIKE?

Establishes the user's morning intake profile and its role in daily energy distribution.

Q3

DO YOU MAKE AN EFFORT TO INCLUDE PROTEIN IN YOUR MEALS?

Evaluates awareness and intentionality in nutrient planning, particularly regarding macronutrient balance.

Meal Structure Preferences

×
Q4

HOW OFTEN DO YOU EAT THE SAME MEALS?

Measures tolerance for repetition and reliance on fixed patterns.

Q5

HOW DO YOUR MEALS USUALLY LOOK ON THE PLATE?

Assesses meal structure and visual consistency in food composition.

Q6

HOW WOULD YOU DESCRIBE YOUR EATING ROUTINE?

Identifies the presence or absence of rhythmic behavior in daily intake.

Flavor Preferences

×
Q7

WHAT KIND OF FOOD COMFORTS YOU MOST?

Explores emotional drivers behind eating behavior.

Q8

WHAT'S YOUR GO-TO SWEET?

Reveals tendencies toward sugar intake and reward-based choices.

Q9

WHAT KINDS OF FLAVORS DO YOU ENJOY MOST OFTEN?

Identifies dominant sensory preferences that may influence meal design.

Each question is linked to a set of visual forms. Every answer is associated with a specific visual form, which together create the user's complete intake shape.

Shapes per Question

Answers are labeled in Q.A format — Question number and Answer number — and each one corresponds to a specific shape.
